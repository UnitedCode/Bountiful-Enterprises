Fabrication

Fabrication Facility

Our fabrication shop consists of 48,000 square feet of enclose building equipped with overhead cranes over every square foot. All finished material is stored inside under a roof until the time it ships to the job site. We have an in-door loading dock where we load trailers with finished material. Our building is also equipped with plumed in welding gas and compressed air throughout the entire building.

Material Handling

We have 3 large yard forkligts for unloading raw material and maintaining our 5 acre material yard. Inside out building we have the following overhead cranes:
(1) 20 ton crane
(3) 10 ton cranes
(6) 50 ton cranes
(4) 30 ton cranes

Python

We purchased this machine new in 2007. This machine has been an exceptional addition to our overall quality and production line. Using files directly from the building's 3D model, it takes the human error out of manually drawing files. Also, using high definition plasma, the cut quality is second to none.

Burn Table

Our burn table was updated in 2014 with full CNC Hypertherm controls and a high definition plasma, allowing us to process plates up to 2-inch thick without sacrificing cut and hole quality. This machine also uses files directly from the building's 3D model to cut parts; taking out the human error of re-drawing files.

Press Break

This 2009 Accupress is the "Cadillac" of press brakes. With a 6 axis fully CNC back-gauge, automatic bench camber, and servo feedback on each ram, a true and straight bend is guaranteed every time. The addition of the CNC bench followers, adds increased safety to the operator while bending large parts.

Band Saw

This HEM Saw is capable of cutting shapes 24W x 16T up to a 60 degree meter. With power in-feed and out-feed rollers and reansfer chains for offloading material. This machine is where we primarily cut all of our hallow structural shapes such as Tubes and Pipes.

Fit-up and Assembly

This process takes the indiveual parts and takc welds them together to create "assemblies". During assembly we have full time inspection persons double checking the fit-up of parts prior to being sent to welding. These inspectors also verify general fabrication of the assembly such as: bolting, cut quality, hole quality, material size, and other dimensional aspects of the assembly. This inspection is recorded using an electronic check list. A seperate check form is created for every assembly.

Welding Process

We are a proud member of the American Welding Society (AWS) Certified Welding Fabricator program. Meaning that we meet and exceed the AWS quality requirements for Welding Fabricators.
We have an In-House AWS Certified Welding Inspector (CWI) to monitor our welding inspection activities and to maintain our procedures and welder qualitfication records.
All welding is performed by certified welders. All welds are visually inspected by a qualified weld inspector. All inspections are recorded on our electronic inspection records. All defects as well as re-work is recorded and attached to the welder and the part number of the individual piece. This system creates very precise records of every part that is welded.
Our primary welding processes is Flux Cored Arc Welding (FCAW) and Gas Metal Arc Welding (GMAW). We have over (20) mig welders ranging in size from 250amp to 400amp. Our welding machines are calibrated annually to ensure correct volt, amp, and wire feed guage readings.

Painting Process

Our pain booths consists of 2 building of 14,000sqft. We also have a 2,500sqft sand blasting shed. All cleaing and painting is done indoors using high volume paint sprayers with draw tubes straight from 55gal drums. Our standard primer is Sherwin Williams rust-inhibiting steel primer. This primer provides ample protection of the material during the construction of the project. Correct paint thickness is verified at the beginning, middle, and end of every shift using a calibrated dry film gauge. This verification is recorded on an electronic record that is attached to the project.
